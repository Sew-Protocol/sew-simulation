┗━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━┛

Phase AB — Per-Dispute Effort Rewards (Phase Y safeguard)       CONFIRMED ✅
  Hypothesis: reward-multiplier ≥ tier-complexity keeps defection < 20%
  Result: HOLDS. Minimum viable multiplier = 2× base fee.
  At 1× (no multiplier): high-complexity tier breaches 20% threshold.
  At 2× or higher: all tiers safe.
  Constraint: reward-multiplier must be at least equal to complexity tier (2× for medium).
  Design recommendation: Implement tiered fee schedule — 1× low, 2× medium, 4× high.

Phase AC — Trust Floor + Emergency Onboarding (Phase Z safeguard)  FAILED ❌
  Hypothesis: trust floor keeps legitimacy ≥ 0.50 across 50 epochs
  Result: DOES NOT HOLD under baseline params (100 disputes/epoch).
  Threshold search (fee × emergency × trust): 0/36 configurations viable.
  Structural constraint found:
    At 100 disputes/epoch with resolver-capacity=15, backlog pressure = 0.667.
    Exit-probability bottoms out at ~84% even with emergency=8 and trust=0.8.
    The trust floor onboards resolvers faster than exits at emergency=8, but
    legitimacy never clears 0.159 — well below the 0.50 collapse threshold.
  Root cause CONFIRMED: constraint is structural, NOT a model artefact.
    Rolling 5-epoch average applied to backlog pressure (April 2026 revision);
    0/36 result unchanged. Pressure at 100 disputes/epoch is persistently high
    (0.667) regardless of smoothing — not a transient spike.
  Design implication: Trust floor requires resolver capacity expansion:
    resolver-capacity × pool-size ≥ 10× disputes-per-epoch
    (option (b) rolling-average already applied and confirmed insufficient)
  Status: Structural constraint confirmed real. Safeguard design requires
    capacity expansion before trust floor can be effective.

Phase AC — Capacity Expansion Simulation                    COMPLETED ✅
  Hypothesis: 10× capacity rule (resolver-capacity × pool-size ≥ 10×
    disputes-per-epoch) makes trust floor viable.
  Result: 10× rule is NECESSARY BUT NOT SUFFICIENT.
  Primary sweep: 0/15 configs viable even at 10× capacity (pressure ≤ 0.10).
  Root cause: cold-start exit spiral.
    Initial resolvers enter with experience ≈ 0.5 (uniform 0.3–0.7).
    trust_score ≈ 0.5 → legitimacy ≈ 0.45 → exit-p ≈ 0.52/epoch.
    Half the pool exits in epoch 1; pressure rises; cascading exits follow.
    Emergency onboarding only triggers after pool has already collapsed.
  Diagnostic results:
    A. Mature pool alone (exp 0.8–1.0) at 10× capacity:  100% pass rate ✅
    B. High trust floor alone (no capacity expansion):    FAILS
    C. Mature pool + high trust floor at 10× capacity:   100% pass rate ✅
  Both conditions required: 10× capacity AND bootstrapped resolver pool.
  Protocol design implication:
    Add a bootstrapping phase before full deployment: operate at light load
    while resolvers accumulate experience (exp ≥ 0.8) before going live at
    full dispute capacity. Trust floor only activates after bootstrapping.

Phase AD — Governance Bandwidth Floor (Phase AA safeguard)      CONFIRMED ✅
  Hypothesis: mandatory floor reviews keep attacker win rate < 20%
  Result: HOLDS at minimum viable configuration.
  Threshold search (floor-reviews × total-capacity):
    Minimum viable: floor=2, capacity=3 → 18.4% win rate.
    Analytical derivation confirmed: floor > n/2 required (floor ≥ 3 of 5).
    Empirical minimum (18.4%) is at the edge — floor=3, capacity=3 is safer (16%).
  Recommended config: floor=3 reviews per epoch, total-capacity=3-4.
  Constraint: floor < n/2 is insufficient regardless of total capacity.
  Design recommendation: Mandate minimum 60% coverage of low-value disputes per epoch.

Phase T — Governance Capture via Rule Drift                    COMPLETE ✅
  Gap tested: Can an attacker with partial governance control (<50% stake)
    drive safety-critical parameters (slash multiplier, appeal bond) below
    viability thresholds via repeated small incremental proposals?

  Model: Each epoch the attacker proposes a -0.05 reduction to slash-multiplier
    (then appeal-bond-bps once slash floor is threatened). Proposals pass when
    attacker votes exceed participating defender votes. Governance timelock:
    5 epochs. Safety floors: slash-multiplier ≥ 1.5, appeal-bond-bps ≥ 200.
    5 seeds × 100 epochs per configuration.

  H1 — Stake threshold (no timing attack, no safeguard):      ✅ HOLDS
    9/9 configurations at stake ≤ 35% produced 0 drift across all abstention
    rates (10–30%). Attacker cannot pass proposals without a majority when
    defenders show up.
    Boundary: breach first appears at stake=45% + abstention=20% (100% fail).
    At 40% stake + 30% abstention: drift=0.75 (surviving but dangerous).

  H2 — Timing attack (abstention spike +20pp every 5th epoch):  ✅ CONFIRMED
    At 40% stake + 20% abstention: timing creates 0.95 drift (vs 0 without).
    At 40% stake + 30% abstention: timing causes breach at epoch ~71
      (same scenario survives without timing — timing converts pass to fail).
    At 45% stake: timing accelerates breach to epoch ~26–34.
    Timing attacks are a confirmed attack vector: a 40% faction can weaponise
    governance participation fatigue to accumulate near-breach drift.

  H3 — Detect-and-revert safeguard:                           ✅ HOLDS
    Auto-reverting one drift step whenever a 10-epoch window shows > 0.10
    cumulative drop completely neutralises the attack even up to 49% stake:
    - stake=0.40, abst=0.30: 0% survival without safeguard → 100% with ✅
    - stake=0.45, abst=0.20: 0% survival without safeguard → 100% with ✅
    - stake=0.49, abst=0.30: 0% survival without safeguard → 100% with ✅
    The safeguard works because drift requires accumulated wins, and each
    reversion forces the attacker to re-win a vote before the next step.

  Protocol design implications:
    Without safeguard: governance safe at ≤ 35% hostile stake, normalabstention.
    With detect-and-revert: safe up to 49% hostile stake (any tested abstention).
    Mandatory recommendation: deploy detect-and-revert for slash-multiplier and
      appeal-bond-bps. For supermajority-gated changes, require 60%+ approval.
    Governance robustness confidence: 50–60% → 80–85% ✅ (gap now closed).
